The Democratic Party Primaries,

What the analysis of newspapers articles can tell us.

   Posted on June 06, 2007

  We all know that what is the most important thing for a political leader, is it's notoriety. It's even better for a politician to be attacked and criticized that to be ignored. Inspired by Presse 2007, I'm launching this project that will allow me to count the number of times each candidates for the Democratic Party primaries for the 2008 election are cited in the US press and see if there is any link the the popularity and, finally, to the primaries results.

The 8 candidates that have launch their campaign so far:


Joe Biden
Hillary Clinton
Christopher Dodd
John Edwards
Mike Gravel
Dennis Kucinich
Barack Obama
Bill Richardson



  I will use a tool I developped and that will soon be available through google hosting facility (code.google.com). This tool download and perform analysis on articles from selected RSS flow.
  The RSS flows I have selected are from the most important US newspapers and deal with all the subjects that matters: politics, national, international, social, economics, education, environment, etc...
  The newspapers are:

 - The New York Times
 - The Los Angeles Times
 - The Washington Post

  If you think of any US newspapers that provide RSS feeds which sufficiently popular to have a large basis of readers, please let me know.

  I'll publish the results shortly.
